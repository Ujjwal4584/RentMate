🔗 How to share RentMate with others

Right now, this website lives as files on your computer. To let anyone else open it using just a link (not a folder of files), you need to put it online — this is called hosting. Here are simple, free ways to do that for a college project:

①

GitHub Pages (most common for college projects)

Create a free GitHub account → make a new repository → upload all your project files (index.html, style.css, script.js, and the pages folder) → go to Settings → Pages → select "main" branch → save. GitHub gives you a free link like https://yourusername.github.io/rentmate/ which you can share with anyone.

②

Netlify Drop (fastest, no account skills needed)

Go to Netlify's drag-and-drop deploy page, drag your whole project folder into the browser window, and it instantly gives you a free working link to share — done in under a minute.

③

Vercel

Similar to Netlify — sign up free, import your project folder or GitHub repo, and Vercel gives you a shareable link automatically.

④

For just showing your teacher quickly

You can also just zip the whole project folder and send it — anyone can unzip it and double-click index.html to open it directly in their browser, no hosting needed. This works for evaluation but won't give a shareable web link.

Once hosted on any of the above, just copy the link it gives you and share it via WhatsApp, email, or your project report — anyone with the link can open and use RentMate from their own browser, on phone or laptop.
